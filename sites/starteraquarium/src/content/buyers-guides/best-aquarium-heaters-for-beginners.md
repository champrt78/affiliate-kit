## How to size a heater: the 5 watts per gallon rule

If you take one number away from this guide, make it this one. A common beginner rule of thumb, cited by hobby sources such as Aquarium Co-Op and Prime Time Aquatics, is roughly 5 watts of heater per gallon of water. A 10 gallon tank wants about a 50W heater, a 20 gallon wants about 100W, and so on. It is a guideline, not a law: a tank in a cold basement may want a little more, and a tank in a warm room a little less. When you are between sizes, sizing up slightly is the safer call, because a heater that runs comfortably is better than one straining at its limit.

That single rule is why this guide spans 50W to 200W across six picks. The preset Tetra HT and a small Eheim or Orlushy cover the 2 to 10 gallon range most beginners and betta keepers start in. The 100W Aqueon Pro lands on a 20 gallon. The Fluval E200 and the 200W hygger reach into the 45 to 65 gallon range for a first community tank that grew. Match the wattage to your gallons first, then choose between simplicity, precision, and durability.
