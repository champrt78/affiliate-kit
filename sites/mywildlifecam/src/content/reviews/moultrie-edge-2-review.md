# Moultrie Edge 2 Review: Nationwide Auto-Connect, No SD Card, a Modest Trigger

## Who This Is For

If your property sits on the edge of cell coverage and you would rather the camera pick the carrier than guess for it, the Edge 2 is the strong pick in its price band. Its headline feature is **nationwide auto-connect**: per the Amazon listing and Moultrie's product page, the camera scans available carriers and locks onto the best one for that location, rather than asking you to commit to AT&T or Verizon at setup the way most dual-SIM competitors do.

It also fits the first-time cellular buyer and the gift buyer cleanly. Built-in memory means there is no class-10 SD card to buy, format, or troubleshoot, which removes the single most common setup headache new cellular-cam owners hit.

## At a Glance

Specs per the Amazon listing and Moultrie's product documentation:

- Photo resolution: 36MP
- Video: 1080p with HD audio
- Connectivity: nationwide 4G LTE, auto-connect across carriers
- Storage: built-in memory (no SD card required)
- Detection range: 100 ft
- Trigger speed: 0.4 seconds
- Viewing angle: 50 degrees
- Flash: low-glow IR
- Price: around $129.99, frequently sold in 2-packs (a 2-pack deal has run near $79, per Men's Journal coverage)
- Buyer rating: 4.1 stars across more than 1,000 Amazon ratings

A cellular data plan is required to send images to your phone. Plan cost is separate from the hardware.

## What It Does Well

- **Nationwide carrier auto-connect.** This is the real differentiator. Most cellular trail cameras in this price range ship dual-SIM and ask you to pick AT&T or Verizon at setup. The Edge 2 selects the best available carrier for the camera's location on its own, which matters most on rural, patchwork-coverage land.
- **No SD card to manage.** Built-in memory uploads straight to the Moultrie Mobile app. There is no class-10 U3 card requirement to get wrong, a requirement that trips up owners of competing cameras like the Tactacam Reveal line.
- **Solid image spec for the price.** 36MP stills and 1080p video with audio are competitive at the roughly $130 single-unit price, and the frequent 2-pack discounting makes the per-camera cost attractive for multi-spot setups.
- **Proven design lineage.** The prior-generation EDGE earned a strong field record: one independent owner who ran two of them for 1.5 years, including a harsh-winter swamp deployment, reported no complaints and roughly two months of runtime on alkaline AAs ([Moultrie EDGE 1 Year Field Review](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2yBxhWvoyc)). The Edge 2 carries the same internal-memory, auto-connect approach forward.

## Where It Falls Short

- **Trigger speed is modest.** At 0.4 seconds per the listing, the Edge 2 is fine for animals that walk through the frame but trails the fastest cameras in the category (the Stealth Cam DS4K Ultimate is rated 0.2s) for catching a fast-moving subject crossing the detection zone.
- **Narrow viewing angle.** The 50-degree field of view is tighter than several competitors, so aiming matters more and a wide trail or food plot may need more careful placement.
- **App friction is a recurring Moultrie theme.** Across the broader Moultrie Mobile user base, app connectivity and notification reliability are the most common complaints. The 4.1-star average across 1,000-plus ratings is good, not class-leading.
- **Subscription is unavoidable for the cellular feature.** Like every cellular camera, the phone-delivery convenience depends on an ongoing data plan. Buyers who balk at a monthly fee are buying the wrong category.

Pick the Tactacam Reveal X when budget is the priority and you do not mind a strict SD-card spec. Pick a non-cellular camera like the Stealth Cam DS4K Ultimate when you want maximum image quality with no recurring fee and you can walk out to swap a card.

## Moultrie Edge 2 vs Edge 3

Buyers shopping the Moultrie Edge series run into the generational question, so here is the practical split. The Edge 2 reviewed here is the current, widely-stocked model: nationwide 4G LTE auto-connect, built-in memory with no SD card, 36MP stills, 1080p video with audio, a 0.4-second trigger, and a 50-degree field of view per the Amazon listing, at roughly $130 single and less in a 2-pack.

Moultrie positions newer Edge-generation hardware above it in the lineup, so an Edge 3 tier steps up on imaging or features at a higher price while keeping the same internal-memory, auto-connect approach. Because the lineup and pricing shift over time, confirm the exact spec sheet and current price for each on Moultrie's product page before buying.

For the buyer who wants the proven no-SD-card, carrier-auto-connect simplicity at the lower price point, the Edge 2 is the value pick in the family. Step up only if the newer generation's added imaging or feature set answers a need you actually have.

## Moultrie Edge 2 vs Spypoint Flex-M

Both are sub-$150-class cellular cameras for the uncertain-coverage property owner, and they solve the carrier problem two different ways. The Edge 2 uses nationwide auto-connect that scans all carriers and picks the best one, with built-in memory so there is no SD card to buy or format. The [Spypoint Flex-M](/reviews/spypoint-flex-m-review) uses dual-SIM auto-carrier-detection across two networks and leads with a free 100-photo-per-month plan, but it stores to an external SD card.

On specs, the Edge 2 lists 36 megapixels and 1080p video against the Flex-M's 28 megapixels and 720p, while the Flex-M's measured 0.35-second trigger edges the Edge 2's 0.4-second rating. The clearest practical difference is storage and plan: the Edge 2 removes the SD card entirely but requires a Moultrie Mobile data plan, while the Flex-M keeps the card but covers light use on its free tier.

Pick the Edge 2 when no SD card to manage and nationwide carrier selection are what you want. Pick the [Flex-M](/reviews/spypoint-flex-m-review) when a free starting plan and dual-SIM coverage matter more and managing an SD card is no obstacle.

## Verdict

If nationwide carrier auto-connect and no-SD-card simplicity are what you value, the Edge 2 is the stronger cellular pick in its price band, because no dual-SIM competitor removes the carrier decision the way it does. If your priority is fastest trigger, widest field of view, or zero subscription, the comparison points elsewhere. The scorecard and comparison above lay out which condition picks which camera.
