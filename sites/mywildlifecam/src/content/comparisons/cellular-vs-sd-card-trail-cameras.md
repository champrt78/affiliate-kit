This is the first real decision in trail cameras, and it is not about megapixels. It is about how you get the photos off the camera. A cellular cam sends them to your phone over a data plan. An SD-card cam stores them locally and waits for you to walk out and grab the card. Everything else is secondary to that one choice.

To make the trade-off concrete we put a flagship of each side by side. The Spypoint Flex represents cellular, with dual-SIM connectivity and a free photo tier. The Browning Strike Force Pro X represents SD-card, with a faster trigger, a smaller body, and no recurring cost at all. Neither is better in the abstract. The right answer is set by how far the camera sits from you and how often you can visit it.

Go cellular when distance or access makes a card-retrieval trip impractical. Stay SD-card when the camera is close, recurring cost bothers you, or you simply want the simplest possible setup with nothing to subscribe to.
