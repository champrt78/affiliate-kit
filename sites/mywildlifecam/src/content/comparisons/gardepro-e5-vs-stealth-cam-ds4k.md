These two cameras sit at opposite ends of the no-subscription SD-card category, so the choice is really about budget against image quality. Both skip the app, the SIM, and the monthly bill. After that they pull apart hard.

The GardePro E5 answers the question a first-time buyer asks, which is whether a working trail cam can cost under $50 without being junk. Three PIR sensors and a no-glow flash put it above the genuinely cheap tier, and at backyard distances the image is fine. The Stealth Cam DS4K Ultimate bets the other way, spending roughly five times as much on a native 4K sensor and a 14-month battery for the buyer who treats image quality as the whole point.

Retrieval distance matters here, because both make you walk out for the card. If the camera sits close and the goal is to find out whether trail cameras are even your thing, the E5 is the low-risk start. If you want the sharpest possible photo from a reachable spot and the budget supports it, the DS4K is the reason to spend more.
