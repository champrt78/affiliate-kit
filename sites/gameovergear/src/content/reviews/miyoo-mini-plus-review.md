## Who This Is For

This is for the player who **lives in 2D and early-3D libraries and wants something pocketable**. The Miyoo Mini Plus is built around classic 8-bit and 16-bit systems through roughly PS1-era games, on a sharp 3.5-inch 640x480 IPS screen in a genuinely pocket-size vertical body. If your sweet spot is **Game Boy, NES, SNES, Genesis, and PS1-era classics**, that is the headline.

It also fits the **tinkerer who wants maximum value**. Miyoo lists a 3000mAh battery plus WiFi and RTC, and the device's reputation rests heavily on community firmware like OnionOS that owners flash to unlock the full experience. The payoff is a capable retro handheld at a price well below Android-based devices.

One thing to settle up front: this is a **2D and early-3D device, not a 3D powerhouse**. Demanding systems like N64, Dreamcast, and PSP sit outside its target range. So this review treats it as what it is built to be, a focused classic-library handheld rather than a do-everything emulation box.

## What It Does Well

- **It nails the classic libraries it targets.** Built around 8-bit and 16-bit systems through roughly PS1-era games, the Mini Plus runs the libraries most people actually replay, which is the core appeal of the device.
- **The screen suits the format.** Miyoo lists a 3.5-inch 640x480 IPS panel, a square-ish resolution well matched to the 4:3 retro games the device is built for.
- **Genuinely pocketable with strong runtime.** The vertical body is small enough to pocket, and Miyoo lists a 3000mAh battery sized for extended sessions, plus WiFi and RTC.
- **Strong value with a deep community.** The price sits well below Android handhelds, and a large community firmware scene, including OnionOS, extends what owners can do with the device over time.

## Where It Falls Short

- **It is not a 3D powerhouse.** Demanding systems like N64, Dreamcast, and PSP are outside its target range. Buy it for 2D and early-3D libraries.
- **The full experience leans on custom firmware.** Much of the appeal comes from flashing community firmware, which is a manual step rather than an out-of-box feature.
- **Vertical and small is a trade.** Great for portability and Game Boy-style play, less comfortable than a larger horizontal handheld for long sessions or heavy shoulder-button use.
- **No official store, and you manage files.** You supply game files yourself, which means sourcing them legally and handling file management.

## Is the Miyoo Mini Plus Worth It in 2026?

For players whose sweet spot is **classic 2D and early-3D libraries, the spec sheet says yes**. The Mini Plus pairs a sharp 3.5-inch 640x480 IPS screen with a 3000mAh battery, WiFi and RTC, and a pocketable vertical body, at a price well below Android handhelds, and it sits on top of a deep community firmware scene. The listing backs the read with a 4.2-star average across 43 ratings on this SKU.

The case against it is specific. It is not built for demanding 3D systems, the full experience leans on flashing custom firmware, and there is no official store. None of those touch the thing the device is bought for, which is **cheap, pocketable classic gaming you are happy to tinker with**. So it is worth it for the 2D-and-early-3D player and hobbyist, not for someone who wants plug-and-play 3D emulation.

## How It Compares

The Miyoo Mini Plus sits in the [Handhelds](/topics/retro-handhelds/) pillar. Two natural comparisons, both spec-sourced:

**Mini Plus vs an Android retro handheld.** An Android-based handheld plays more demanding 3D systems and offers a plug-and-play, app-store experience, at a higher price and larger size. The Mini Plus is a focused, pocketable 2D-and-early-3D device built around custom firmware at a much lower price. Choose the **Mini Plus for cheap, pocketable classic gaming**, an Android handheld for 3D systems and minimal setup.

**Mini Plus vs a horizontal handheld.** A horizontal device offers full-size shoulder ergonomics and a more comfortable long-session grip, often with a bigger screen. The Mini Plus trades that for true pocketability in a vertical body. Pick the **vertical Mini Plus for portability**, a horizontal handheld for comfort on longer sessions.
