These two sticks rarely cross-shop on price alone, because they sit in different tiers. The choice is platform reach and wireless value against tournament-grade parts and a wired competition build.

The 8BitDo Arcade Stick is the value-and-flexibility pick. Switch and Windows support over 2.4GHz wireless, Bluetooth, or USB-C, plus 8BitDo's remap software and a tournament-mode lock, are the reasons it shows up on so many casual short lists. You accept standard parts rather than premium ones to land the price.

The Hori Fighting Stick Alpha bets the other way. Arcade-quality parts, replaceable artwork, and an openable panel on a wired PS5, PS4, and PC build are the reasons competition-minded players pay more than double. You give up Switch support and wireless in exchange for the parts and the pedigree.
