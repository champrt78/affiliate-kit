These two clone consoles both play original cartridges, so the choice is not about whether they accept your carts. It is about how they play them and how much you want the box to do.

The Hyperkin RetroN 5 is the feature-rich pick. Wide cartridge coverage across NES, SNES, Genesis, and the Game Boy family, plus HDMI output, save states, and on-screen filters, are the reasons it tops multi-cart short lists. You accept an emulation-based approach, which is the accuracy trade purists weigh, in exchange for the breadth and convenience.

The Retro-Bit Super RetroTRIO answers with a simpler three-in-one for NES, SNES, and Genesis, built around hardware-based playback. You give up the Game Boy support and the on-screen features in exchange for a more straightforward, hardware-driven design.
