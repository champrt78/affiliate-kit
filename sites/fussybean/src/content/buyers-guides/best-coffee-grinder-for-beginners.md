## Burr vs blade, conical vs flat, stepped vs stepless: what matters for beginners

The grinder terminology sounds fussier than it is. Three plain distinctions cover almost everything a first-time buyer needs.

**Burr vs blade.** A blade grinder spins a propeller that chops beans into a random mix of powder and pebbles. That unevenness is why blade-ground coffee tastes muddy: the dust over-extracts into bitterness while the chunks under-extract into sourness, in the same cup. A burr grinder presses beans between two surfaces and crushes them to a consistent size you can actually choose. Every pick in this guide is a burr grinder, because moving off a blade is the upgrade most beginners notice first.

**Conical vs flat.** This describes the shape of the burrs. Conical burrs, a cone sitting inside a ring, are the standard at beginner prices and appear on five of the six picks here. They are forgiving and cheaper to manufacture. Flat burrs, two facing rings like the MiiCoffee DF54's 54mm set, are prized by espresso enthusiasts for a tighter, clearer grind, but they cost more and are not a requirement for a first grinder. For most beginners, conical is the sensible default and flat is the later step.

**Stepped vs stepless.** This is how the grinder changes coarseness. A stepped grinder clicks between fixed positions, which makes a setting easy to return to but can leave you stuck between two clicks when chasing a precise espresso grind. A stepless grinder, like the DF54, slides continuously for fine control. The Timemore C3S Pro lands in between with roughly 36 levels, close enough to stepless for most uses. Outside espresso, stepped is perfectly fine; espresso is the one job where stepless control starts to earn its keep.

A note on authorities: James Hoffmann's "A Beginner's Guide to Coffee Grinders" is a useful primer, and it is worth being precise about what it does. He walks through representative grinder categories and states he does not necessarily recommend specific models, so we cite him for the category framing above, not for any single product pick.
