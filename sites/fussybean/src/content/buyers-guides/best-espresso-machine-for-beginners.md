## Who this is for

This guide is for someone buying their first real espresso machine: stepping up from a pod system, or starting from scratch, and trying to figure out where the money should go. The picks above are compared on the specs that actually decide whether a beginner machine was a good buy, boiler type, portafilter size, whether a grinder is included, and footprint.

## How these were chosen

We compared current manufacturer specifications, aggregated patterns from verified-buyer reviews on Amazon, and independent testing where it exists. We did not test these machines ourselves; this is comparison-and-fit analysis. See [how we evaluate](/how-we-evaluate/) for the full rubric.

## The short version

If you want the fewest separate purchases, the all-in-one Breville Barista Express is the convenience default. If you would rather buy a machine you can modify and keep for years, the Gaggia Classic Pro with its 58mm commercial portafilter is the enthusiast path. The De'Longhi Dedica wins on price and counter space; the Breville Bambino Plus wins if automatic milk is the priority.
