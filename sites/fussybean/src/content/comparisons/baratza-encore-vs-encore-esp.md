These two grinders share a body, a burr set, and a reputation, so the choice is not about quality. It is about one question. Do you pull espresso, or are you ever going to?

The original Baratza Encore is the entry standard for filter coffee. Its 40 stepped settings are spaced for the range where drip, pour-over, French press, and cold brew live, and owner reports lean on its consistency and its long serviceable life. What it does not do well is espresso, because its finest setting was never built to reach that far.

The Encore ESP answers the other half of the room. It keeps the same burrs and re-gears the adjustment into a dedicated espresso range and a filter range, so the fine end actually reaches espresso. It costs more and ships with a dosing cup. Our [Baratza Encore ESP review](/reviews/baratza-encore-esp) digs into how that retuned range behaves at the fine end. The original saves money for a filter-only setup, the ESP buys headroom for the day you add a machine.
