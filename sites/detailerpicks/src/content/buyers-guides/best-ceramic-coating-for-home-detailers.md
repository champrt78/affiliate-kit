## Spray, kit, or wipe-on: what the categories mean

Consumer ceramic products sort into three tiers by how you apply them and how long the maker claims they last.

- **Ceramic sprays (toppers).** Turtle Wax, Meguiar's, and Adam's here are the easiest to use. You either spray and buff with a microfiber or, in Meguiar's case, spray onto wet paint and rinse off with no buffing and no cure time. Their manufacturers position them as toppers: the protection is real but rewards periodic re-application rather than lasting years untouched. This is the lowest-effort, lowest-cost way into ceramic protection.
- **A step-up spray-on coating.** Gyeon CanCoat EVO is a true SiO2 coating that happens to apply like a spray: you mist it onto a microfiber and wipe it across the panel. Its listing claims 12 months of protection, putting it between a topper and a wipe-on kit in both effort and durability.
- **Wipe-on enthusiast coatings.** CarPro CQUARTZ UK 3.0 and Gtechniq Crystal Serum Light take the most prep and the most careful application. You decontaminate the paint, wipe the coating on in sections, and level it. In return their listings carry the longest durability claims here: 24 months handwash-only for the CarPro kit and up to 5 years for Gtechniq. CarPro's 70% SiO2 figure is the only published silica percentage in this set.

There is no single correct tier. The right pick depends on how much time you want to spend applying and re-applying, and how long you want the protection to last between sessions.
