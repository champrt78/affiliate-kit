These two do the same job and bleed the same telltale purple, so the choice is rarely about whether they work. It comes down to two trade-offs. Reputation and aggressive bleed versus a milder smell and a lower price.

CarPro IronX is the formula most reviewers benchmark against, and that reputation is earned. It reacts hard on heavy fallout and shows up in nearly every serious decontamination guide. The catch is the sulfur smell that arrives as it works, which is genuinely strong in an enclosed space.

Adam's Iron Remover lands the same purple-bleeding result with a smell that owner reports describe as much easier to tolerate. For detailers who already shop the Adam's lineup, that plus the lower price is usually enough. Neither replaces a clay bar. Both belong in the decontamination step before you seal the paint.
