Both of these land in the same price bracket and both are pH-balanced, so the choice is rarely about safety or cost alone. It comes down to two trade-offs. Ready-to-use convenience with color-changing feedback versus a dilutable concentrate that costs less per wash.

The Adam's spray is the no-thinking option. You point it at a dirty wheel, it clings and turns purple as it lifts iron, and you rinse. The Diablo Gel asks for one extra step, a dilution, in exchange for a bottle that lasts much longer and a thick gel that holds onto vertical barrel faces.

Owner reports for both cluster around the same praise, that they cut brake dust without harsh acid fumes. The deciding factor for most home detailers is workflow. If you wash a couple of cars now and then, the spray is friendlier. If you wash often and watch your supply cost, the concentrate wins.
