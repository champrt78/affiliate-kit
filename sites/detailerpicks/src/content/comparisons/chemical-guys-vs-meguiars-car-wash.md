These are two of the highest-volume car wash soaps on the shelf, and on the surface they look interchangeable. They are not. The difference is what each one is designed to leave behind.

Mr. Pink is a pure shampoo. It cleans, it foams, and it rinses away with nothing left on the paint. That makes it the right call when you are washing to prep, before clay, before polish, or before laying down a fresh wax or coating, because anything left on the surface would get in the way.

Gold Class goes the other direction. It carries paint conditioners that boost gloss and feed existing wax as you wash, which is great for maintaining a car that is already protected and that you want to keep looking warm. The same conditioners that help a finished car are exactly what you do not want right before decontamination. For most home detailers the honest answer is that these are not rivals so much as two tools, one for prep and one for maintenance.
