# DetailerPicks — SEO Keyword Map

**Site:** detailerpicks.com — car-detailing products for home detailers / enthusiasts (wash soap, ceramic coatings, foam cannons, drying towels, interior cleaners, wheel cleaners).
**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Goal:** buyer-intent long-tail keywords a newer-authority site can realistically rank for and monetize via Amazon.

## Method (no paid volume tool)

- **Google autocomplete** harvested live from the public suggest endpoint (`google.com/complete/search?client=chrome&q=...`) across ~21 seeds (use-case, surface, car-type, comparison, safety, price, product-specific). This is real demand data, not recall.
- **SERP reads via WebSearch** to grade winnability: who actually ranks? Brand .coms + huge forums + established review sites = HARD. Small detailing blogs (jimbosdetailing, fresh-layer, canadiangearhead, carwashwiz), Reddit, niche forums, thin pages = WINNABLE.
- Winnability replaces a difficulty score. HIGH = small-blog/forum SERP we can out-publish. MED = mixed (brand product pages own the slots but the "which one / comparison" angle is open). LOW = mega-sites or one entrenched competitor owning every result.

## The single sharpest finding (read this first)

**"for home detailers" is brand positioning, not a search query.** Nobody types it. Every autocomplete pull confirms the real long-tail demand lives in **use-case / surface / car-type / comparison** modifiers:
- soap → `for ceramic coating`, `for foam cannon`, `for black cars`, `ph neutral`
- ceramic → `for black cars`, `for beginners`, `vs wax`, `coating vs spray`
- wheel cleaner → `for brake dust`, `for black wheels`, `safe for ceramic coated wheels`
- towel → `for ceramic coated cars`, `for black cars`
- foam cannon → `for pressure washer`, `for garden hose`, `do i need special soap`
- interior → `for leather seats`, `for car leather and plastic`

Every existing title currently spends its most valuable real estate (the modifier slot) on a phrase with ~zero search volume. The retrofits below redirect that slot toward what people type, without throwing away the existing rankings.

## Keyword-to-URL ownership rule (avoid self-cannibalization)

**One owner per long-tail. Don't target the same term on two URLs at once.** A contested modifier lives as an H2/FAQ *inside the existing guide* until it proves enough demand to spin out as a standalone page — then it graduates, and the guide's H2 internal-links up to it. Four terms below are deliberately assigned to the existing guide first (the new-piece table flags them as "graduate later"):
- `ceramic spray for beginners` → ceramic guide H2 now (new-piece #5 is the eventual spin-out)
- `ceramic spray for black cars` → ceramic guide H2 now (new-piece #4 = spin-out)
- `wheel cleaner for ceramic-coated wheels` → wheel guide H2 now (new-piece #9 = spin-out)
- `interior cleaner for leather and plastic` → interior guide retrofit now (new-piece #10 = spin-out)

## 1. Per-Existing-Page Retrofits

### `best-car-wash-soap-for-home-detailers`

Brands own the product slots; the *neutral comparison* slot is open to us. The `ph neutral` and `foam cannon` sub-angles are HIGH (small blogs + forums only).

## Notes / honesty caveats

- **Volume is inferred, not measured.** Autocomplete presence proves a query is *typed enough to be suggested*, and ordering roughly tracks popularity, but there's no absolute number here. Treat the priority ranking as intent × winnability, not as a volume forecast.
- **Brand .com dominance is real on the head terms.** Chemical Guys, CARPRO, GYEON, The Rag Company, etc. own the *product* slots. We don't beat them on "buy [their product]" — we win the **comparison / which-one / is-it-safe-for** intent they structurally can't serve from a single-product page. Every recommendation above leans into that gap.
- **Jimbo's Detailing is the recurring small-blog competitor** (beginners, black cars, drying towels, foam-cannon soap). It's beatable (small site, thin internal linking) but it's entrenched on several of these — expect to need a genuinely better-structured page, not a thinner clone.
- **All retrofit H2/FAQ suggestions are answerable under the comparison-and-fit voice doctrine** — every one is framed from manufacturer specs, listing claims, or buyer-review summaries, never a hands-on-use claim. No suggestion here requires us to say we tested anything.
