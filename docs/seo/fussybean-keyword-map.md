# FussyBean — SEO Keyword Map

**Site:** fussybean.com — home coffee gear for beginners + coffee novelty/gifts
**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Method:** Google autocomplete (`suggestqueries.google.com` JSON endpoint) + WebSearch SERP inspection. No paid volume tool — winnability is judged by *who actually ranks page 1*, not a difficulty score. Volumes are deliberately omitted (no tool = no fabricated numbers); priority = intent strength × winnability.

**Winnability key:**
- **HIGH** — page 1 is small/mid coffee blogs, Reddit, brand pages, Q&A forums. No Wirecutter/NYT/CNN/Serious Eats. A new-authority site can crack it.
- **MED** — mixed: some big sites (CNN Underscored, HuffPost, Tom's Guide, Gear Patrol) but several thin/small pages alongside. Rankable with a genuinely better, more specific page.
- **LOW** — owned by major authority + commerce giants. Skip the head term; chase the qualified long-tail instead.

All comparison/"worth it" terms below were chosen because **we already have the product on a page** — the retrofit just surfaces the keyword in title/H2/FAQ. That is the cheapest possible SEO win: content exists, only the addressing is missing.

- **Year tokens matter here.** Autocomplete repeatedly surfaces `(2026)` / `2026` on roundup terms. Keep the `(2026)` in guide titles and bump `lastUpdated` so the freshness signal holds.
- **`reddit` is the #1 or #2 autocomplete suffix on almost every coffee term.** That confirms the niche trusts forum opinion over brand copy — our comparison-and-fit, owner-report-citing voice is the right register to win these. It also means Reddit threads *are* the competition on many tails (good news: a well-structured page outranks a thread).
- **No volumes by design.** Every label here is SERP-observed, not estimated. If a paid tool is added later, validate the MED calls first (`under $200` espresso, `coffee gifts for men`) — those are the ones where a big-site presence could push them to LOW.
