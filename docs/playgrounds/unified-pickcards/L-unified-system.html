The full detail

The "ranking" here is not about which product cleans best in absolute terms — most premium interior detailers clean adequately for weekly maintenance. The ranking reflects which reader segment each product fits best, based on documented chemistry and credible third-party reviewer commentary.

The one-bottle workflow: Chemical Guys InnerClean →

Chemical Guys' product page lists InnerClean as compatible with leather, plastic, vinyl, cloth, chrome, glass, carpet, metal, upholstery, rubber, and LCD screens. The combination of broad surface compatibility, matte OEM finish (no greasy sheen), and one-step cleaner-plus-protectant chemistry makes it the strongest single-product match for the reader who wants a workflow that fits in one bottle.

The Autopia forum thread comparing InnerClean to 303 noted it leaves "no residue like 303," meaning users perceive it as drier on the surface. The 4.7-star Amazon rating across over 6,000 reviews on the 16 oz variant is the strongest social proof in this lineup.

The category UV benchmark: 303 Aerospace Protectant →

303 Aerospace is the category UV-protection benchmark. Gold Eagle (the manufacturer) documents SPF 40 and up to 98% UV block, claiming the highest UV protection of any surface protectant in the category. Larry Kosilla (AMMO NYC) and the AutoGeek detailing community consistently reference 303 as the dedicated protectant standard.

Important framing: 303 is a protectant, not a cleaner. It is applied to a clean surface, not to a dirty one. Pair with Chemical Guys InnerClean for the cleaning step, then apply 303 to the dashboard and door cards for the UV-protection step.

Best for the show-car owner wanting a polymer layer

SiO2 ceramic-infused, no carnauba sheen: Adam's Total Interior Cleaner →

Adam's Total Interior is the technical differentiator in this lineup. The Amazon listing calls out SiO2 ceramic-infused technology, and the product page describes a "self-crosslinking polymer" that cures into a coating-like film rather than evaporating like a typical silicone protectant. The result: matte finish on hard non-porous surfaces, foaming action on textiles.

The trade-off: AutoGeek and AdamsForums threads note Adam's interior line is regarded as well-engineered but premium-priced compared to Meguiar's for equivalent surface cleaning. The polymer-coating angle is the reason to pick Adam's over InnerClean.
