What this is. Google has discovered these pages but hasn't crawled/indexed them yet — normal for new, low-authority domains. Manually requesting indexing nudges them to the front of Google's crawl queue. It's a push, not a cure (the durable fix is backlinks + links from MWC), but it's free and it helps.

How to do each one

  1. Open Google Search Console and select the property for the site you're working (each site is its own property).
  2. Paste a URL from the list below into the search bar at the very top ("Inspect any URL in…"). Click the row's Copy button or click the URL text to copy it.
  3. Wait for the inspection to finish, then click Request Indexing.
  4. It runs a quick live test (~30–60s), then queues the page. Tick the checkbox here and move to the next.
📊
~10 per day, per site. Each site is a separate property with its own daily quota — you can do 10 on each of the three the same day. If it says "quota exceeded," stop and resume tomorrow.
🚫
Don't re-submit. Requesting the same URL twice does nothing and wastes a slot. Tick it here so you don't repeat it.
⏭️
Money pages only. Legal/utility pages (privacy, disclosure, contact, about) and the homepage are intentionally left off — they don't need to rank and would waste your 10 slots. Google finds them on its own.
⏳
Be patient after. Indexing can take days to weeks on a young domain, and some may get indexed then dropped until the site earns authority. That's expected — don't re-request.
