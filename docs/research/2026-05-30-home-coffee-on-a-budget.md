## The four-piece cheap-start kit (validated)

### 1. Manual burr grinder — TIMEMORE Chestnut C2S
- **Brand:** Timemore
- **ASIN:** `B0CGTYT12R` — discovered via WebSearch ("Timemore Chestnut C2 manual burr coffee grinder amazon"), confirmed by browser load. H1: "TIMEMORE Chestnut C2S Manual Coffee Grinder Stainless Steel S2C Conical Burr Coffee Grinder, Capacity 25g Hand Coffee Bean Grinder, Double Bearing Positioning, Black."
- **Price (2026-05-30):** $75.00 (buybox).
- **Rating:** 4.7 out of 5 across 930 Amazon ratings; listing shows "100+ bought in past month."
- **Sourced specs (Amazon listing + Timemore copy):** CNC stainless steel S2C conical burr; 25g capacity; internal adjustable setting with double-bearing positioning; compact hand grinder marketed for pour-over, French press, and travel.
- **Why it's the kit's grinder:** A burr grinder is the single piece that changes the cup most, and a manual one is the cheapest path to real burr quality (no motor to pay for). The base C2 listings were out of stock; the C2S is the in-stock sibling with the upgraded S2C burr.

### 2. Brewer — Bodum Brazil 34oz French Press
- **Brand:** Bodum
- **ASIN:** `B000KEM4TQ` — discovered via WebSearch ("Bodum Brazil 34 oz French press coffee maker amazon"), confirmed by browser load. H1: "Bodum 34oz Brazil French Press Coffee Maker, High-Heat Borosilicate Glass, Black - Made in Portugal."
- **Price (2026-05-30):** $19.99 (buybox; listing showed a -27% mark from a $27.50 list price).
- **Rating:** 4.5 out of 5 across 19,834 Amazon ratings; "700+ bought in past month."
- **Sourced specs (Bodum listing copy):** Borosilicate glass carafe, three-part stainless steel mesh plunger, 34oz / 8-cup capacity, made in Portugal. Bodum's own copy: "good design doesn't have to be expensive."
- **Why it's the kit's brewer:** No paper filters, no electricity, no learning curve beyond a timer. The French press is the canonical cheap, forgiving first brewer. AeroPress is the alternative for single-cup drinkers; this kit picks the press for batch capacity at the lowest price.

### 3. Kettle — Bonavita 1L Variable-Temperature Gooseneck
- **Brand:** Bonavita
- **ASIN:** `B005YR0F40` — discovered via WebSearch ("Bonavita gooseneck variable temperature electric kettle amazon"), confirmed by browser load. H1: "Bonavita 1L Digital Variable Temperature Gooseneck Electric Kettle for Coffee Brew and Tea Precise Pour Control, 6 Preset Temps, Café or Home Use, 1200 Watt, LED Panel, Stainless Steel."
- **Price (2026-05-30):** $99.99 (buybox).
- **Rating:** 4.4 out of 5 across 7,195 Amazon ratings.
- **Sourced specs (Bonavita listing copy):** 1L capacity, 1200W, six preset temperatures plus single-degree variable control, gooseneck spout for pour control, 304 stainless steel.
- **Honesty flag for the writer:** This is the one piece where cheap and good diverge. At ~$100 it is the priciest item in the kit. The narrative should be honest: the kettle is optional for a press-only start (any kettle or a stovetop pot works), and a plain stovetop gooseneck runs far less. The Bonavita earns its place only once a buyer wants pour-over temperature control. Do not pretend it is the cheapest possible kettle.

## Cost-math sources (informational backbone)

All figures attributed; none invented. Used for the cafe-vs-home and cost-per-cup sections.

- **Cafe annual spend:** Reporting aggregated across consumer-cost write-ups puts the average daily cafe habit near $1,100/year (about 275 lattes at roughly $3+ each), and a $6–$8 specialty-drink habit at $1,500+/year. (Apartment List "Save Money on Coffee"; 247 Calculator "Coffee Habit Cost Calculator," 2025.)
- **Home cost per cup:** Commonly cited at under $0.50/cup, with the at-home brewer landing near $85/year versus roughly $985/year for the cafe buyer. (Low Key Coffee Snobs "Cost of Coffee at Home vs. the Coffee Shop"; 42 Days Coffee "Coffee Shop vs Home Brewing.")
- **Per-cup gap:** Typically $2–$4 saved per cup brewing at home vs. cafe drip. (Same sources.)
- **Use these as ranges, attributed to "published cost comparisons," not as precise universal truths.** Bean choice, milk, and local cafe pricing move the numbers a lot.
