# Research note — Best Coffee Grinders for Beginners (fussybean)

Factual research backing a 6-product beginner-grinder buying guide. Every ASIN was discovered from a Firecrawl web/search result URL (not recalled), then confirmed by scraping the Amazon `/dp` page and reading the H1 product title. The one exception is the Baratza Encore ESP, whose ASIN was already validated in the existing fussybean review (`sites/fussybean/src/content/reviews/baratza-encore-esp.md`) and is reused here as an intentional cross-link. Prices and stock are as observed on the scrape date 2026-05-29 and will drift.

Voice note for the writer: no hands-on claims anywhere below. All sentiment is attributed (Amazon listing data, manufacturer copy, named reviewers). This internal note uses em dashes for readability, but the published copy must scrub them (and other AI-tells) per the fussybean voice doctrine. Critically: do NOT attribute any specific product ranking to James Hoffmann — see the Encore ESP note; he teaches grinder categories rather than naming a single best model.

## 1. Bodum Bistro Electric Conical Burr Coffee Grinder

- **Brand:** Bodum
- **Validated ASIN:** `B0043095WW` — discovered via Firecrawl search ("Bodum Bistro electric burr coffee grinder amazon"), confirmed by `/dp` scrape. H1 reads: "Bodum Bistro Electric Conical Burr Coffee Grinder, Preset Timer, 12 Grind Settings, Black." Single buyable unit.
- **Price + source:** $49.99 new (buybox JSON on the scraped `/dp` page, 2026-05-29). A used buying option at $44.61 is also listed; the guide should reference the new price.
- **Segment / use-case it wins:** The cheapest real burr grinder in the set. The pick for a beginner who is still on a blade grinder and wants the single biggest upgrade for the least money. Note the listing also offers a *blade* Bistro (ASIN `B0043073WQ`) at a similar name; that one is excluded on purpose because it is not a burr grinder.
- **Sourced specs:**
  - Stainless steel conical burrs that crush rather than chop, to preserve aroma. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW "About this item")
  - "Over 14 settings from coarse to fine," espresso to French press. Note the H1 says "12 Grind Settings" while the bullet says "over 14" — flag this discrepancy; use "around 12 to 14 stepped settings" in copy. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW)
  - Borosilicate glass catch container, marketed to reduce static cling. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW)
  - Preset timer. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW H1 + listing)
- **Attributed reviewer notes:**
  - Amazon rating: 3.7 out of 5 stars across 8,351 global ratings — a large, mixed sample. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW, 2026-05-29)
  - Amazon listing positions it as a "Premium Burr Grinder" style at an entry price, but the lower star average signals the trade-off at this price point. (Amazon `/dp` B0043095WW product details + ratings)
  - Its stepped 12-14 range and entry burr set mean true espresso fineness is a stretch; treat espresso capability as marginal rather than a selling point. (Inference from the listed spec range; do not present as a quote.)
- **AVAILABILITY:** In Stock as of 2026-05-29 (some buybox states showed "Only 1 left in stock" intermittently. Verify at scaffold time).

## 3. Timemore Chestnut C3S Pro Manual Coffee Grinder

- **Brand:** Timemore
- **Validated ASIN:** `B0CC4Z32F8` — discovered via Firecrawl search ("Timemore Chestnut C3 manual coffee grinder amazon"), confirmed by `/dp` scrape. H1 reads: "TIMEMORE Chestnut C3S Pro Manual Coffee Grinder, Stainless Steel S2C Conical Burr Coffee Grinder, Hand Coffee Grinder with Foldable Handle, Adjustable Grind Setting for Travel/Camping/Home, Black." Single buyable unit.
- **Price + source:** $95.00 (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8, "Price ($95.00)", 2026-05-29).
- **Segment / use-case it wins:** The manual hand grinder. Best pick for a beginner who wants notably better grind quality than a budget electric, has no counter space or budget for an electric, or wants travel portability. The S2C burr is a step up from the older C2.
- **Sourced specs:**
  - S2C660 patent hexagonal conical burr, 55-58 HRC hardness, 5-axis CNC machined, for uniform grind with minimal fines. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8 "About this item")
  - All-metal aluminum alloy body (upgrade from the plain C3), for durability and heat dissipation. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8)
  - Approximately 36 adjustable coarseness levels via the adjustment nut, fine to coarse. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8)
  - Foldable crank handle plus a built-in bearing for labor-saving, momentum-carrying grinding; compact ~52mm body. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8)
- **Attributed reviewer notes:**
  - Amazon rating: 4.8 out of 5 stars across 1,644 global ratings — the highest-rated pick in this set. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8, 2026-05-29)
  - The Chestnut C-series is widely discussed as a budget-manual value benchmark; a public r/espresso thread covers its fine-grind settings for espresso. (Reddit r/espresso, surfaced via Firecrawl search; treat as community discussion, not a single named reviewer.)
  - Manual operation is the trade-off: espresso-fine grinding is possible but effortful versus an electric. (Amazon `/dp` B0CC4Z32F8 listing positioning.)
- **AVAILABILITY:** In Stock as of 2026-05-29. NOTE: the base non-Pro "Chestnut C3" (`B09TP85KCJ`) was checked and is **Currently unavailable**, which is why this guide uses the buyable C3S Pro variant instead. The C3S MAX (`B0BZHFCJQT`, $105, 35g capacity) is an in-stock alternate if a larger-capacity manual is wanted.

  - 150W AC motor; aluminum body with powder-coat finish. (MiiCoffee manufacturer page spec table)
