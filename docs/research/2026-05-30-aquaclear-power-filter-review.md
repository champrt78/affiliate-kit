## Verified specs (from the live Amazon listing feature bullets)

- Flow: **300 GPH** (gallons per hour), listed in the product detail spec.
- Three-stage filtration: **AquaClear Foam (mechanical) + Activated Carbon (chemical) + BioMax (biological)**, plus a **Cycle Guard** dose that protects the biological bacteria colony.
- **Filtration volume up to 7x larger** than comparable HOB filters — the media basket holds more media for longer contact time. This is the refiltration / contact-time design point.
- **Flow control lever** on the intake U-tube: adjust water flow; set to maximum for initial start-up (per the listing's setup instructions).
- Energy-efficient pump; manufacturer recommends cleaning the filter every 2 weeks for best operation.
- Mechanical + chemical + biological filtration all in one unit.

## Pro themes (attributed)

- **Huge media basket / long contact time** — manufacturer: 7x filtration volume vs comparable HOB filters; this is the AquaClear's signature design.
- **Customizable media** — Amazon verified buyer notes you can run the filter without the activated carbon (unlike cartridge filters that force a monthly cartridge buy), saving on recurring media cost.
- **Three-stage filtration + Cycle Guard** — manufacturer: mechanical, chemical, biological in one unit.
- **Flow control lever** — manufacturer: adjustable flow, useful for bettas / small fish that dislike strong current.
- **Reliability reputation** — 4.4 stars across 7,712 ratings (Amazon).

## Con themes (attributed, honest)

- **Bare open intake, no built-in surface skimmer or pre-filter sponge** — small fish / fry / shrimp can be drawn toward the intake. Grounded by Aquarium Co-Op selling a dedicated intake adapter so owners can add a pre-filter sponge; their buyer review notes the stock inlet tube holds a sponge loosely. Frame as a design fact + common owner mod, not personal experience.
- **Flow can feel modest at the rated tank ceiling** — one Amazon verified buyer with a 29 gallon and messy fish felt 300 GPH was on the low side for the upper end of the range; informs sizing up (the 50 over the 30) when stocking is heavy.
- **Priming / start-up step** — per the listing's own setup instructions you fill the case with water and set the flow lever to max for initial start-up; it is not strictly self-priming out of the box. Frame as a setup note.
- **Cleaning cadence** — manufacturer recommends cleaning every 2 weeks for max efficiency; routine maintenance like any HOB.
