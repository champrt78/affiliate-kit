# Fellow Stagg EKG Electric Kettle, Research Note

## TOP-LEVEL FINDING (read first, orchestrator decision needed)

The product the brief names, the **original / standard "Stagg EKG" electric kettle (the pre-Pro generation with the monochrome LCD, no scheduling, no app)**, no longer exists as a buyable SKU. It has been **discontinued and replaced by the Stagg EKG Pro generation** on both Amazon and Fellow's own store. Evidence:

- Amazon: a full 110-result sweep for "Fellow Stagg EKG Electric Gooseneck Kettle" returns **only Stagg EKG Pro** electric listings (every electric Fellow gooseneck title contains "Pro" and "Scheduling") plus the unrelated Stagg **Stovetop** kettle. No non-Pro electric EKG listing exists.
- Fellow's own product page (`/products/stagg-ekg-electric-pour-over-kettle`) is titled "Stagg EKG Electric Kettle," but its **Model selector offers only "Pro" and "Pro Studio Edition."** There is no standard model to add to cart.

So the iconic kettle sold today under the "Stagg EKG Electric Kettle" name **is the Pro**. This note documents the current Pro (the real buyable product) and flags the generation switch loudly. It does **not** relabel the Pro as the discontinued standard. The orchestrator should decide whether to (a) publish this review of the current Pro under the fussybean slug, or (b) reframe the piece title to "Pro." Recommendation: publish as a review of the current Stagg EKG (Pro), since that is what a buyer who searches "Fellow Stagg EKG" will actually find and buy.

## Known flaws / limitations (sourced)

- **Price premium.** $179.95 on Amazon ($199.95 direct), well above commodity gooseneck kettles. Even a fan calls Fellow "on the pricey side ... the BMW or Mercedes of gadgets" (Amazon owner), and Lowkey frames the comparison set as "any kettle a pour-over snob might reasonably comparison shop with," not budget kettles. Sources: https://www.amazon.com/Fellow-Stagg-Electric-Gooseneck-Kettle/dp/B0BF7DXLBF and https://www.lowkeycoffeesnobs.com/fellow-stagg-ekg-review/
- **Generation / Pro vs standard vs app confusion.** The product line has churned: the original standard EKG, the EKG Pro, the Pro Studio Edition, plus the non-gooseneck Corvo and the stovetop Stagg. Fellow's own app note adds confusion: the "EKG Updater" app is firmware-only and "does not currently support remote control of your kettle or its settings," so buyers expecting phone control will be disappointed. Source: https://fellowproducts.com/products/stagg-ekg-electric-pour-over-kettle
- **Boil-over / "geysering" from the flat lid (third-party, attributed).** Lowkey: overfilling can cause water to "spurt out the vent holes," attributing it to the flat lid (vs a concave lid that gives roiling water somewhere to go): "a roiling boiling there is SCARY!" Mitigation: stay under the fill line. Source: https://www.lowkeycoffeesnobs.com/fellow-stagg-ekg-review/
- **Handle can get warm (third-party, attributed).** Lowkey notes the handle attaches "through a metal ring right next to the metal kettle body," so "if any of your fingers slip a bit, they're liable to get a little uncomfortably hot." Source: https://www.lowkeycoffeesnobs.com/fellow-stagg-ekg-review/
- **Small capacity for multi-cup sessions.** 0.9 L (about three 12 oz mugs per an Amazon owner). One reviewer of a competitor explicitly wanted "something bigger" than "Fellow's 900mL." For larger batches Fellow points buyers to the XL Clyde. Sources: https://www.amazon.com/Fellow-Stagg-Electric-Gooseneck-Kettle/dp/B0BF7DXLBF and https://www.lowkeycoffeesnobs.com/fellow-stagg-ekg-review/
- **Customer-support friction (attributed owner complaint).** One Amazon owner who damaged the kettle base reported Fellow's "Chat with us," "Phone Appointment," and "Submit a Support Request" channels were all "fruitless ... BUYER BEWARE." A single account, but worth flagging given the premium price. Source: https://www.amazon.com/Fellow-Stagg-Electric-Gooseneck-Kettle/dp/B0BF7DXLBF
- **Not the fastest boiler.** An owner notes it "doesn't work as fast as our Cuisinart," trading raw speed for design and precision. Source: https://www.amazon.com/Fellow-Stagg-Electric-Gooseneck-Kettle/dp/B0BF7DXLBF

## Who this is for / who should skip

- **For:** Home pour-over and specialty-tea drinkers who want to-the-degree temperature control, a true gooseneck for slow controlled pouring, and a temperature-hold that keeps water at target through a multi-step brew. Strong fit for someone who treats the morning brew as a ritual and values design on the counter (the recurring praise is "looks good, works well, feels intentional"). The Pro's Guide Mode presets and brew stopwatch help beginners; the all-steel body and wood-handle options suit the warm, cozy fussybean aesthetic.
- **Skip if:** You want a cheap kettle that just boils water (this is a premium-priced precision tool), you need large capacity (0.9 L is small, look at Clyde or a 1.5 L+ kettle), you expect phone/remote app control (the app is firmware-only), or you want maximum boil speed over design and precision. Anyone shopping the discontinued original standard EKG should know it is no longer sold; the current product is the Pro.

## How it fits a pour-over / home-coffee setup

For a fussybean reader building a pour-over station, the Stagg EKG (Pro) is the water half of the core trio: grinder, kettle, dripper. Its job in the workflow is temperature precision and pour control. Set a target (Fellow's range is 104-212°F, with to-the-degree control), let the kettle hit it, and use Hold Mode (15/30/45/60 min) so the water stays on-temp through the bloom and the staged pours rather than drifting cold mid-brew, exactly the pain an Amazon owner and the Lowkey review both call out as solved here. The precision gooseneck is what separates it from a standard kettle: a slow, steady stream lets the user wet the grounds evenly for a balanced extraction, and the built-in brew stopwatch lets them keep the pour-over on a repeatable timeline. It pairs naturally with a burr grinder and a V60/Kalita-style dripper on the counter, and the steel-plus-wood finishes read as the warm, tactile "third-place café" look fussybean is going for. The honest counterweight for the reader: it is small (about three mugs) and priced like a design object, so it is the right pick for the daily ritual brewer, not the office break room.
