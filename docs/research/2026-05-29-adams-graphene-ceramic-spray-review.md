## Sourced spec facts and claims

Every claim below is from a retrieved source (the brand product page or the live Amazon listing), cited inline. No memory specs.

1. **Chemistry: a graphene-oxide spray coating, positioned by Adam's as a "true" graphene coating versus silica-infused products.** The Amazon listing states: "In the car care industry, ceramic coatings and nano technology have become buzzwords... although sometimes effective, these are not true 'ceramic coatings'. They are silica infused products that behave like a coating. Adam's has created the next evolution of ceramic coatings creating a legitimate spray graphene coating." This is Adam's marketing framing, attributed as such, not an independent chemistry finding. Source: https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ
2. **Durability: Adam's states ">9H Hardness, 1+ years or more of Protection."** This is the only sourced durability figure for this product and it is the manufacturer's claim. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
3. **Hydrophobics: contact angles averaging 110-118°, with a lower sliding angle such that water evacuates at angles of less than 10-15°.** Manufacturer-stated. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
4. **Application method: spray-and-wipe.** Adam's instructions: prep with clay bar/mitt and (optionally) a paint correction step, then Surface Prep to remove oils; spray the product into a microfiber applicator or premium towel; wipe onto the panel; let it sit 1 to 2 minutes; buff to a high shine with a premium microfiber towel; allow 4 hours to cure before exposure to the elements. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
5. **Work panel by panel.** Adam's recommends applying one panel at a time rather than spraying the whole car at once, to keep overspray under control and ensure thorough coverage. Sources: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2 and https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ
6. **Cure / flash time: lets it "rainbow" and flash for 1 to 2 minutes before removal, then keep the surface element-free for roughly 3 to 4 hours.** Adam's notes the product starts curing as the trigger is pulled and the product flows onto the towel. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
7. **Layering: up to about 3 applications is useful; beyond that is redundant.** Adam's FAQ: "any application after 3 rounds will render the product redundant and still provide the same hydrophobic effects," and recommends waiting about 1 hour between layers, while generally not recommending layering because coatings struggle to bond to themselves once curing starts. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
8. **Surfaces: safe on painted surfaces, wheels, glass, headlights, plastic trim, bed liners, tonneau covers, canvas tops, floor mats, chrome, and unfinished metal.** Sources: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2 and https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ

Adam's explicitly states this is an application aid only and that lack of glow does NOT indicate lack of coating (it is not a durability indicator). Sources: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2 and https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ

## Known flaws / limitations (sourced)

- **Streaking, especially on glass and on light-colored paint (critical owner review, attributed).** One Amazon reviewer: "I have found this stuff and the gold version streak. I wasn't sure because my vehicle is silver. I did the windows which streaked. The shine is not so great... The slickness was poor!" and reported preferring other products (Optima Plus, Technicians Choice, Last Coat). A real dissenting voice against the easy-to-apply consensus. Source: https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ
- **High spots if a section is missed or over-applied.** Adam's own FAQ acknowledges this: if you miss a spot, catch it within 10 to 20 minutes and reapply; if left longer, a high spot may need Brilliant Glaze or even machine polishing to remove. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
- **Prep is non-negotiable for best results.** Adam's leads its instructions with clay-bar/decontamination and surface prep; the coating bonds to a clean, decontaminated surface, so skipping prep undercuts both durability and finish. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
- **Air-shipping ban.** Cannot ship via air (overnight, 2-day, priority, or international) when ordered direct, so do not expect fast shipping from the brand store. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
- **"DO NOT SMELL" warning** appears in Adam's own feature list, an offhand flag that the product has a strong odor and should be used with ventilation. Source: https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2
- **It is a spray coating, not a multi-year professional coating.** An owner described it plainly as "not a heavy-duty true ceramic coating." The manufacturer's durability claim is "1+ years or more," which sits well below a professional installed coating's multi-year service life. Sources: https://www.amazon.com/Adams-Graphene-Ceramic-Spray-Coating/dp/B08CPDDGZZ and https://adamspolishes.com/products/graphene-ceramic-spray-coating%E2%84%A2

## Who this is for / who should skip

- **For:** Weekend enthusiasts and home detailers who want a fast, spray-and-wipe layer of graphene/ceramic protection without the working time, leveling, and risk of a professional installed coating. Good for someone who already does the prep (wash, clay, decon) and wants strong water beading, added gloss, and roughly a year of protection from a $35 to $40 bottle that covers multiple cars. The UV tracer is a genuine help for first-timers who worry about missing spots. Also a reasonable topper to refresh an existing ceramic coating.
- **Skip if:** You want a true multi-year coating (this is a spray, manufacturer-rated around a year, not a 5-plus-year installed coating). Skip if you will not do the prep work, since the finish and durability depend on it. Be cautious if you are coating a lot of glass or very light paint, given the streaking complaint from at least one owner. Skip if you enjoy regularly waxing or glazing your car, since owners report wax and glaze stop adhering once this is applied. And if you need it shipped fast direct from Adam's, note the no-air-shipping restriction (buying via Amazon avoids that).

## How it fits a coated-car maintenance routine

For a reader who already runs a full ceramic-coated car (DetailerPicks' ceramic-coated-car-owner segment), position this product two ways, both sourced to Adam's own framing. First, as a **standalone protective layer** for a daily driver or a second car that does not warrant a professional install: prep, apply panel by panel, and get roughly a year of hydrophobic protection and gloss for the price of a bottle. Second, as a **topper / booster** over an existing coating, which Adam's explicitly supports ("Great as a stand-alone product or topper for your current Ceramic Coating," and it can go over the top of any standard ceramic coating). In a maintenance rhythm, that means a coated car gets its routine two-bucket wash, then a periodic reapplication of this spray to restore beading and slickness as the base coating ages, rather than stripping and reinstalling. One owner's note that wax and glaze no longer adhere afterward is worth flagging to the reader: once this is on, the maintenance step becomes "keep it clean and re-spray," not "wax it."
