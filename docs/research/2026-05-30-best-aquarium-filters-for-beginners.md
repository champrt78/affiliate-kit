## Notes / gotchas surfaced during research

- Amazon `/dp` rawHtml ships ALL availability state strings ("In Stock", "Currently unavailable", "Only N left") in the DOM regardless of actual state, so a naive grep for "In Stock" is a false-positive trap. The reliable signal is presence of `id="add-to-cart-button"` + `id="buy-now-button"` AND the `primary-availability-message` span carrying `a-color-success` text, cross-checked against `OutOfStock` in the buy-box region. This is how the Marina S15 and Cascade 300/600 were caught.
- `firecrawl scrape` writes the page under top-level `.rawHtml` and `.metadata` (no `.data` wrapper in this CLI version) — extract title via `.metadata.title`.
- `grep -P` fails under the default locale here ("supports only unibyte and UTF-8 locales"); use `LC_ALL=C grep -oaiE` for binary-ish HTML.
