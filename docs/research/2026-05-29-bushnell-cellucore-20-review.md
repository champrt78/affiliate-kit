# Bushnell CelluCORE 20 — Research Note

Single-product review research for mywildlifecam.com (niche: cellular trail cameras). All specs and quotes below are pulled from retrieved sources and cited inline. No memory-sourced specs. No hands-on claims.

## ASIN confirmation (and the variant/model tangle)

**Confirmed canonical buyable ASIN: `B098KPX5B4`** — the AT&T-carrier CelluCORE 20.

How confirmed: Firecrawl search surfaced the listing at `/dp/B0CM9JKT4B` (URL slug reads "Verizon," which is misleading). Scraping that page shows it **canonicalizes to `B098KPX5B4`** — the scraped `metadata.url` resolves to `https://www.amazon.com/Bushnell-CelluCORE-Trail-Camera-Verizon/dp/B098KPX5B4?th=1`, and `B098KPX5B4` appears 109 times in the page body versus only 2 for `B0CM9JKT4B`. So:

- **`B0CM9JKT4B`** is a child/variant ASIN that redirects to the parent.
- **`B098KPX5B4`** is the canonical listing with the AT&T variant selected (`?th=1`). Its buybox shows the AT&T unit in stock at $113.94.
- The page also exposes a **Verizon** carrier variant in the swatch at **$108.99** ("Only 6 left in stock").

This is the **single-carrier AT&T** SKU, not a dual-SIM unit. Evidence:
- Amazon product-details table lists Model Name **"Bushnell 119904A: Cellucore 20 At&t, Brown"** and the H1 title is "Bushnell CelluCORE 20 Trail Camera **for AT&T** with Low Glow/80ft Night Range and HD Video" [Source 1].
- Amazon sells **separate carrier-locked SKUs at different prices** (AT&T $113.94, Verizon $108.99) — carrier locking is the opposite of dual-SIM.
- A separate eBay listing calls it "Bushnell **A20** CelluCore 20MP **AT&T** Cellular Trail Camera" [Source 5], and Runnings sells a dedicated "Bushnell Cellucore 20 Trail Camera, **AT&T**" SKU [Source 6]. Independent retailers confirm the AT&T-only unit is real.

**Model-number discrepancy worth flagging (open question):** Bushnell.com no longer lists a single-SIM CelluCORE 20. Its current product page is the **"CelluCORE 20 Dual SIM"**, Product ID **BU-119904D** [Source 2] — a *different* model from the Amazon unit's **119904A**. The Dual SIM is a newer/separate SKU that "automatically connects to the strongest network signal AT&T or Verizon." The Amazon AT&T listing (119904A) is the older carrier-locked variant, now moving largely through third-party sellers. **Do not import the Dual-SIM marketing specs onto this ASIN as if they were the same product.** For this review's reviewed unit, lean on the Amazon listing (119904A) and the TrailCamPro test of the "a20" variant.

> Prior MWC research flagged "single-carrier AT&T-only" for B0CM9JKT4B. That flag was **correct** for this ASIN. The dual-SIM framing belongs to the separate 119904D model on Bushnell.com.

## Sourced flaws / limitations

- **Customer service reputation is mixed-to-poor.** Sourced from an Amazon review describing an hour-plus hold with no answer [Source 1], and a second review citing "Zero support from seller or Bushnell" (RO47UAQOC7LR9). This corroborates the prior MWC research flag candidly, now with citations.
- **Single-carrier lock (AT&T).** The reviewed ASIN is locked to AT&T (119904A). Buyers in Verizon-strong areas need the separate Verizon SKU; neither auto-switches. Only the newer Dual SIM 119904D auto-selects [Source 2]. Coverage at the buyer's specific site is the deciding factor.
- **No on-camera viewer/setup screen** — setup is app-only [Source 3].
- **Reliability drift reports** — several owners describe units that worked initially then began repeating frames or false-triggering [Source 1].
- **Interpolated 20 MP** — the headline megapixel figure is software-interpolated, not native resolution [Source 3].
- **Average detection circuit** and **night illumination practical to ~50-60 ft** despite the 80 ft headline [Source 3].
- **Thin third-party stock** — sold by "toys texas," only 2 units showing for the AT&T variant [Source 1]; affiliate-link stability is a concern.
- **Flash-type ambiguity** — listing says "Low Glow," independent testing classifies it Red Glow IR, which is more visible to animals and people than no-glow [Sources 1, 3].

## Who it's for / who should skip

**For:** Budget cellular-cam buyers in confirmed AT&T-coverage areas who want simple, affordable Bushnell data plans (TrailCamPro notes "Bushnell cell camera plans are simple, affordable, and there is an unlimited option" [Source 3]) and value good daytime image quality and easy app setup over premium speed.

**Skip if:** you are in a Verizon-only or marginal-AT&T area (buy the Verizon SKU or a dual-carrier camera instead); you need a no-glow flash for pressured game or security; you want fast back-to-back captures (40.3 s photo recovery is slow); or responsive manufacturer support is a priority given the sourced service complaints.

## How it compares to dual-SIM rivals

The CelluCORE 20 (119904A) is **single-carrier (AT&T)**, which is its core competitive weakness against auto-/dual-carrier rivals that pick the strongest local signal automatically:

- **Spypoint Flex** — markets dual-carrier auto-connect (LTE picks the best of multiple networks), removing the need to guess the buyer's carrier before purchase. For buyers unsure of local coverage, that flexibility is the headline advantage the AT&T-locked CelluCORE lacks.
- **Moultrie** (e.g. Edge/Mobile line) — markets auto-connect across carriers as well. Again, the differentiator over the AT&T-locked CelluCORE is not having to buy the right carrier SKU.
- **Bushnell's own CelluCORE 20 Dual SIM (119904D)** — Bushnell's current page states it "automatically connects to the strongest network signal AT&T or Verizon" [Source 2]. If a buyer wants the Bushnell ecosystem without carrier lock-in, the Dual SIM is the in-family upgrade; the AT&T 119904A on Amazon undercuts it on price but not on flexibility.

Note: rival auto-connect/dual-SIM claims above are framed as **manufacturer marketing positioning** for comparison context; specific Spypoint/Moultrie spec numbers were not independently retrieved in this pass and should be pulled before any head-to-head spec table is published.
