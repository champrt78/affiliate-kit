Source-attributed research for detailerpicks piece #4 (Best Wheel Cleaner for Home Detailers).

## Category-level signal (Reddit + reviewer consensus)

1. **P&S Brake Buster is the pro-detailer default for heavy-duty routine wheel cleaning.** Pan The Organizer named it his 2023 Wheel & Tire Cleaner Award winner across 30+ product categories. Appears in "what do pros use" discussions as the economy-at-scale answer. Dilutes 5:1 for weekly maintenance, undiluted for heavy contamination. Source: pantheorganizer.com 2023 Product Awards.

2. **Sonax Full Effect is the community iron-killer reference.** Color-change behavior (yellow-green to deep red/purple) is treated almost as a ritual by enthusiasts. The aspirational mid-market iron-killer pick. Available at 500ml (~$16.99) and 5L sizes. Source: sonaxusa.com, Amazon B003UT3S6Q reviews.

3. **Bilt Hamber Auto-Wheel is the "enthusiast import" — gaining US ground but with real availability friction.** Strong on UK forums; US availability limited to Detailed Image (1L) and a 5L Amazon US listing (B08H5VBBSZ). No 1L Amazon US listing confirmed. Source: Amazon.com search, detailedimage.com.

4. **pH-neutral is the community default doctrine; acid cleaners treated as specialist tools.** Forum consensus on AutoGeek and Autopia is that acid-based cleaners are unnecessary for most home detailers and are a genuine risk on coated, anodized, or polished finishes. Every product in this candidate set is pH-neutral or pH-balanced. Source: autogeekonline.net forum.

5. **Iron-reactive (color-change) formulas are favored for visual proof, not necessarily superior cleaning power.** Community acknowledges that the color-change reaction (thiocarbamate chemistry) indicates iron contamination but that non-color-changing formulas like Brake Buster achieve equivalent brake dust removal through different chemistry — with the added benefit of depositing a corrosion-inhibiting layer. Source: autogeek forum, detailedimage.com Ask a Pro reviews.

## Doctrine splits

### 1. pH-neutral vs acidic
Every candidate product in this set is pH-neutral or pH-balanced; that is the correct default for a home-detailer guide. Acid-based wheel brighteners (e.g., acid-type alloy cleaners) are aggressive on brake dust and mineral deposits but create genuine risk on clear-coated, ceramic-coated, anodized, or polished finishes. The piece should state this as primary doctrine and never recommend an acid cleaner without explicit finishes safety gating. Note: Meguiar's Hot Rims G9524 (high-alkaline, not acid) still carries significant surface safety limitations despite not being acidic.

### 2. Iron-killer (color-changing) vs basic cleaner
Iron-reactive chemistry (thiocarbamate compounds) provides visual proof of ferrous contamination removal and is the enthusiasm-driving feature in this category. However, non-color-changing formulas like P&S Brake Buster remove brake dust effectively through surfactant/foaming action and leave a corrosion-inhibiting protective layer. Community split: iron-killer for periodic deep decon or show-car sessions; Brake Buster (or diluted Diablo) for weekly maintenance where economy and corrosion protection matter more than visual feedback. The guide should surface this as a routine vs. decon workflow choice.

### 3. Gel vs spray-on
Gel concentrates (Diablo CLD_997, Brake Buster) cling to vertical wheel surfaces longer, reducing run-off waste and giving chemistry more contact time. Spray-on RTU formulas (Sonax Full Effect, CarPro Iron X, Meguiar's G180124) are faster for weekly washes but may run off vertical surfaces before full dwell completes. For a home detailer doing a dedicated wheel-cleaning session with a wheel brush, gel format is preferable. For quick maintenance washes or foam cannon use, diluted spray concentrate wins. The Meguiar's G180124 splits the difference with a gel-like spray consistency.

### 4. Coated-wheels-safe vs not
Critical split for detailerpicks' ceramic-coated-car-owner segment. Safe: Sonax Full Effect, Bilt Hamber Auto-Wheel, P&S Brake Buster, Meguiar's G180124, CarPro Iron X (all confirmed pH-neutral or pH-balanced, explicit coating-safe claims). Not safe: Adam's Wheel Cleaner (explicit manufacturer warning: do not use on anodized aluminum); Meguiar's Hot Rims G9524 (not safe on polished, anodized, or powder-coated). Any product with an acid component must never be recommended for coated wheels.

## Synthesis for the buying guide

The wheel cleaner category splits cleanly across two use patterns: routine weekly maintenance cleaning and periodic iron decontamination. The strongest anchored picks for detailerpicks' audience are **P&S Brake Buster as the daily-driver and pro-shop default** (dilutable, economical at scale, all-finish safe, deposits corrosion-inhibiting protection, no sulfur odor, pan-community trusted) and **Sonax Full Effect as the enthusiast iron-killer reference** (color-change feedback is a ritual for this audience, pH-neutral, explicitly ceramic-coat safe, the category's consensus standard). The accessibility/value entry point is **Meguiar's Ultimate All Wheel Cleaner G180124** — the only iron-reactive color-changing formula in the ~$10 mass-retail tier. Bilt Hamber Auto-Wheel is the recommendation for the UK-aware enthusiast or Detailed Image customer, but US availability friction makes it a secondary pick that requires a direct note about where to buy it. CarPro Iron X belongs in the piece as a "periodic deep decon" callout (2-4x/year) rather than a routine weekly cleaner, paired with a note that it is Pan The Organizer's iron remover pick. Adam's Wheel Cleaner fills a brand-loyalty slot but must carry a clear anodized-surface limitation disclaimer if included.
